In my robotics club freshman year, I was immediately designated as the main coder in our group because of my familiarity with programming Arduinos. While building our robot, I was enchanted by my role. Programming was an orchestra: the code, compiler and the computer were the musicians, and I, the conductor, commanded a symphony in the key of C++. Robotics, however, only marked the beginning of my journey in programming. I became engrossed in many personal projects, where each project was built from the pyramid of knowledge gained from the previous while also implementing new technology. I went from making a simple hangman program in ninth grade to fully developing a natural language processing AI that summarizes texts. My passion for computer science steadily grew; each project pulled me deeper into the rabbit-hole of computer science. The hole seemed endless; at the end of every concept I would learn, two more would appear to take its place. Despite the endless ordeal, I love digging past the layers of abstraction and falling deeper into the realm of computer science.
